StudentSwap

A Platform for Student Items Exchange

Interactive Prototype

Problem Statement

"How might we enable university students to safely and confidently trade their unwanted personal belongings, while also providing an avenue for students in need to acquire new items at little to no cost, reducing the stress of accumulating unnecessary items?"

University students often face challenges finding items they need from people they trust. Online platforms like Facebook Marketplace are often prone to scams and fraudulent activities.

Solution

StudentSwap addresses this by offering an exclusive marketplace for buying, renting, and selling other items to verified students. Our core functionalities include categorised listings, real-time notifications, and a unique request feature that allows students to post items or need requests, especially when urgent.

Mindmap

We used a mind map to quickly ideate and explore the app's potential features, participants, challenges, and objectives without delving into technical details.

Sketches

Hand-drawn sketches were created independently by team members to visualize core features and user flows, followed by collaborative discussions to align our vision and establish a foundation for wireframing.

Wireframes with Information Architecture

We developed mid-fidelity wireframes in Figma based on our sketches, focusing on central features while establishing a foundation for high-fidelity prototyping and design consistency across screens. Also with information architecture to ensure a logical and user-friendly navigation structure.

Observational Techniques

We evaluated the system using Think Aloud and Post-Task Walkthrough methods to capture real-time user insights and understand design challenges:

  • Think Aloud: Participants verbalised their thoughts and decision-making processes while interacting with the system, allowing us to capture real-time insights into user navigation and confusion points.
  • Post-Task Walkthrough: After completing tasks, participants discussed their recent actions to articulate their reasoning and help us understand why certain design elements were challenging.

Results

What Worked

  • Pretty intuitive interface
  • Easy to make a request to other students
  • Addresses the need for a safe and trustworthy marketplace
  • Create a more efficient and responsive student marketplace

Limitations

  • Learning Curve: Incorporate an onboarding process with tutorials to guide users through creating listings, viewing requests, and messaging features.
  • Limited Form Information: Expand the listing form to include item condition, brand, and preferred payment methods (cash, bank transfer).
  • Demand Validation: Further testing needed to validate demand and assess whether users prefer using this app over alternative solutions.

Future Improvements

  • Integrate social features like follower systems and community groups
  • Add real-time notifications for listings matching user interests
  • Enable community-based groups tied to student accommodations
